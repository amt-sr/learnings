8.2.8 Replication (Kafka Replication)

Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐☆☆ Interview Frequency: ★★★★☆

Goal

Understand how Kafka replicates topic partitions across brokers for fault tolerance, the leader-follower model, and the role of the In-Sync Replicas (ISR) set.

Definition

Kafka replication copies each partition of a topic across multiple brokers. One replica serves as the leader (handles all read/write requests), and the others serve as followers (passively replicate from the leader). If the leader broker fails, one of the in-sync followers is elected as the new leader, ensuring data availability and fault tolerance.

Key Concept: Replication provides durability and availability. Messages are not considered fully acknowledged until they are written to the leader and (depending on acks setting) replicated to followers. If the leader fails, an in-sync follower takes over with no data loss.

Leader-Follower Model

Leader

  • Handles all read and write requests for the partition.
  • Producers always write to the leader (unless a configuration error).
  • Consumers always read from the leader.
  • Maintains the authoritative log for the partition.

Followers

  • Pull messages from the leader's log to stay in sync.
  • Do not serve client requests (read or write) unless they become leader.
  • If the leader fails, an in-sync follower is elected as the new leader.

Replication Factor

The replication factor controls how many copies of each partition exist across the cluster:

Replication FactorCopiesTolerates FailuresStorage Overhead
11 (leader only)0 broker failures1x
21 leader + 1 follower1 broker failure2x
31 leader + 2 followers1 broker failure (with acks=all)3x
Interview Tip: "What replication factor do you use?" A common answer is 3 — it provides fault tolerance against one broker failure while maintaining availability (the partition remains writable even after losing one broker). The replication factor should not exceed the number of brokers in the cluster.

Write Path with Replication

Producer writes to Partition 0 with acks=all: 1. Producer ──write──► Leader (Broker 1) Leader writes to local log 2. Leader ──replicate──► Follower 1 (Broker 3) Follower 1 writes to local log, sends ACK to leader 3. Leader ──replicate──► Follower 2 (Broker 2) Follower 2 writes to local log, sends ACK to leader 4. Leader has received ACK from all ISR replicas Leader ──ACK──► Producer (success) If Broker 2 is slow: Follower 2 takes too long → removed from ISR Leader only waits for ISR members (Broker 1 + Broker 3) → Still returns success to producer

Leader Election

When the leader broker for a partition fails:

  1. A controller broker (one of the brokers in the cluster) detects the failure.
  2. The controller selects a new leader from the ISR for that partition.
  3. The new leader starts serving read/write requests.
  4. Followers begin replicating from the new leader.
Unclean Leader Election: If no ISR members are available (all followers are too far behind or also failed), Kafka can elect a non-ISR follower as leader. This results in data loss for messages the new leader did not replicate. This is configurable (unclean.leader.election.enable) and is generally disabled in production to prevent data loss.
