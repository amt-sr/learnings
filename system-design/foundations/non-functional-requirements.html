Non-Functional Requirements (NFRs)

Goal

Understand:

  • What Non-Functional Requirements (NFRs) are.
  • Why they are more important than Functional Requirements for architecture.
  • Common categories of NFRs.
  • How NFRs influence system design.
  • How to identify NFRs during interviews.

Definition

Non-Functional Requirements describe how well a system should perform rather than what it should do.

They define the quality attributes, constraints, and operational characteristics of a system. They often drive architectural decisions more than functional requirements do.

Examples:

  • 99.99% availability
  • Response time < 200 ms
  • Support 10 million daily active users
  • Encrypt data at rest and in transit
  • Survive a regional outage

How NFRs Affect Architecture

RequirementPossible Architectural Decision
Low latencyRedis, CDN, caching
High availabilityReplication, multiple regions
ScalabilityLoad balancers, sharding
DurabilityWAL, replication, backups
SecurityTLS, encryption, OAuth
ReliabilityCircuit breakers, retries

Typical Interview Questions

  • What availability target are we designing for?
  • Is eventual consistency acceptable?
  • What is the expected latency?
  • How many daily active users?
  • What read/write ratio should we assume?
  • Should the system survive regional failures?
  • Are there compliance requirements?
  • Is the system globally distributed?

What to Skip

Don't spend time on:

  • IEEE SRS templates
  • Formal Requirements Engineering processes
  • UML Use Case diagrams
  • Enterprise requirement management tools
  • Academic classifications of NFRs

These have low ROI for system design interviews compared to understanding how NFRs influence architectural decisions.
